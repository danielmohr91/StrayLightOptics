About

The objective here is to provide some computational tools that might help to improve understanding or intuition about stray light fundamentals. I’ve also assembled a list of references that have been pertinent to the projects that I’ve been involved with, and as such, is more of a sampling of the current literature, rather than a comprehensive survey.
This website is intended to be primarily informational for engineers involved with evaluating stray light. It is not a one-stop-shop for everything related to stray light analysis. You’ll find an FAQ on the basics that will help to ensure you are not on the wrong flight. There’s a reference section with links that I’ve run across in my work. And there is a collection of graphical and computational toys that are useful for first order calculations, and that can help improve intuition and understanding of first order principles. For engineers just getting started with all this, I’ve also included resources that will give you a leg up.

Who I am

I’ve been doing stray light analysis since 1982, starting out in the aerospace industry, then joining Breault Research Organization, which at the time was one of the foremost stray light house in the world. I’ve evaluated stray light on star trackers, missile systems, space-based and land-based telescopes, high-power fusion lasers, consumer optics and sensors, and wafer inspection equipment operating in the DUV. Since 1998, I have also been responsible for teaching stray light analysis to physicists and optical engineers.
